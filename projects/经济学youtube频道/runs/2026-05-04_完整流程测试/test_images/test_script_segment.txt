When the Bretton Woods system collapsed in 1971, a new unspoken hierarchy emerged: the United States could print dollars without gold backing, and the world still needed dollars to buy oil. Saudi Arabia agreed to price all oil sales in dollars, and in return the U.S. offered military protection and weapons. Dollars earned from oil had to be recycled back into U.S. Treasuries — creating perpetual demand for American debt. This arrangement became known as the petrodollar system.
